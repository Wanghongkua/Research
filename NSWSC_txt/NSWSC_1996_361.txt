

Regina versus R M M [1996] NSWSC 361 (19 August 1996) 
REGINA versus RMM /95 
MONDAY 19 AUGUST 1996 
IN THE COURT OF CRIMINAL APPEAL 
MAHONEY P, McINERNEY J, HULME J 
CATCHWORDS: Criminal law - Sexual assault - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether verdict unsafe and unsatisfactory - Corroboration - Evidence of children - Inconsistencies in complainant's evidence 
RESULT: DISMISSED 
CRIMINAL LAW - Corroboration - Appellant convicted of sexual assault on niece pursuant to <case> [Crimes Act ](http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/nsw/consolact/ca190082/) - Appeal against conviction on grounds verdict unsafe and unsatisfactory -
Uncorroborated evidence of child complainant - Problems with children's evidence in criminal proceedings - Importance of absence of corroboration in case of sexual offence against a young person - Effect of [Evidence Act ](http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consolact/ea199580/) [s ](http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consolact/ea199580/s164.html), which removed requirement for corroboration - Nature of issue to which corroboration would go was whether the assault took place at all - Advantage of jury in seeing witnesses - Held (by majority) the facts of the alleged assault and the lack of corroboration of themselves are not sufficient to warrant this Court concluding that the jury or this Court must have had a reasonable doubt as to whether the assault occurred: 
Shotter versus Friend [(1690) Carth 142]; Browne v
Collins [(1872) 21 WR 222]; R versus Lillyman [(1896) 2 QB 167]; R versus Lovell [(1923) 17 CrAppR 163]; R versus Dossi (1918) 13 CrApprR 158; R versus Hester [(1973) AC 297]; Pollitt versus R <case>; [(1992) 174 CLR 558]; referred to.

Fabre versus Arenales [(1992) 27 NSWLR 437], applied.

Chambers versus Jobling [(1986) 7 NSWLR 1]; Devries v
Australian National Railways Commission <case>; [(1993) 177 CLR 472]; referred to.

CRIMINAL LAW - Identification evidence - Issue not raised at trial - Duty of trial judge to ensure accused has a fair trial - Held (by majority) it was open to the jury to conclude that the complainant had sufficiently and accurately identified the appellant as her assailant: 
Pemble versus The Queen <case>; [(1971) 124 CLR 107]; Howe versus The Queen [(1981) 55 ALJR 5]; Ward [(1989) 42 ACrimR 56]; Smith & Kirkton [(1990) 47 ACrimR 43]; Vella [(1990) 47 ACrimR 119]; Domican versus R <case>; [(1992) 173 CLR 555]; referred to.

CRIMINAL LAW - Inconsistencies in witnesses' accounts - Inferences to be drawn - Whether inconsistencies in complainant's evidence such as would lead a jury to conclude witness was not telling the truth - Held (by majority, Hulme J dissenting) appeal dismissed, on the evidence the jury could conclude that the charge was established beyond reasonable doubt, therefore appellate court not warranted in interfering with the jury verdict - Observations (by Mahoney P) on the dangers of convicting an accused person on uncorroborated evidence of a witness of young age: 
M versus The Queen [(1994) 181 CLR 487] applied.

R versus O'Brien (NSW Court of Criminal Appeal, 5 November 1993, unreported); R v Telford Justices, Ex parte Badhan [(1991) 2 QB 78]; referred to.

ORDERS 
Appeal against conviction and sentence dismissed.
